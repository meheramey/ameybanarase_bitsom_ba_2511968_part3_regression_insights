# Part 3: Regression-Based Business Insights & Model Interpretation

## 1. Business Problem Summary
The leadership team of our retail chain wants to mathematically determine the primary drivers behind monthly sales performance across various retail storefronts. To optimize operational budgets and maximize top-line revenue, leadership is evaluating critical strategic interventions. These include adjustments to marketing spend, modifications to discounting strategies, improvements in inventory availability thresholds, staff reallocations, and the prioritization of specific store formats or regional territories. 

This analysis leverages ordinary least squares (OLS) linear regression models to isolate variables statistically correlated with sales volume, distinguish pure association from physical causation, and deliver data-backed recommendations for capital allocation.

---

## 2. Dataset Description & Variable Taxonomy (Task 1)
The baseline data is stored under `data/business_regression_data.xlsx`. A comprehensive pre-modeling audit was conducted to classify variables, identify required transformations, and filter out non-predictive metadata.

### A. Target / Dependent Variable ($Y$)
* **`monthly_sales`**: Continuous numerical variable representing the total gross revenue generated by an individual store location within a given monthly fiscal cycle. This is the core metric the regression model aims to predict and optimize.

### B. Potential Independent Variables ($X$)
* **`marketing_spend`**: Continuous numerical variable tracking localized promotional and advertising capital injected into the store's market.
* **`footfall`**: Continuous numerical metric measuring the total volume of customer traffic entering the physical store space.
* **`avg_discount_pct`**: Continuous numerical value tracking the average rate of markdowns and promotional discounts offered.
* **`inventory_availability_pct`**: Continuous numerical metric indicating the percentage of stock keeping units (SKUs) maintained in a shelf-ready, available status.
* **`customer_rating`**: Quantitative metric captured from post-purchase feedback channels acting as a proxy for customer satisfaction.
* **`staff_count`**: Discrete numerical field capturing total human resource allocation on the shop floor.
* **`region`**: Categorical textual variable capturing the geographical territory of the store (e.g., North, South, East, West).
* **`store_type`**: Categorical textual variable defining the operational format of the store location (e.g., Express, Supermarket, Flagship).

### C. Technical Classifications & Modeling Pre-conditions
* **Pure Numerical Variables:** `marketing_spend`, `footfall`, `avg_discount_pct`, `inventory_availability_pct`, `customer_rating`, and `staff_count`.
* **Pure Categorical Variables:** `region` and `store_type` (These require dummy variable engineering before entering a multi-variable linear regression equation).

### D. Variables Requiring Cleaning, Transformation, or Imputation
* **Percentage Fields (`avg_discount_pct`, `inventory_availability_pct`):** Must be cross-verified to ensure mathematical uniformity. If stored as text or whole numbers (e.g., `15` instead of `0.15`), they must be structurally cast into clean continuous floats.
* **Null / Missing Values:** Any rows containing blank spaces across our key predictors must be isolated. Rows with corrupted fields will be systematically omitted on a listwise basis to preserve model integrity.
* **Outlier Truncation:** Extreme data entries within `footfall` or `marketing_spend` driven by technical logging glitches will be audited to prevent artificial skewing of our baseline regression coefficients.

### E. Non-Predictive Variables (Excluded from Regression)
* **`store_id` / `order_month`**: Unique alphanumeric or primary key string identifiers. While critical for indexing, database joins, and tracking file lineage, these attributes exhibit zero statistical variance linked to structural market behavior and are dropped from the active regression matrix to avoid over-fitting and mathematical noise.
